About Our Academy


Welcome to Amdanga Taekwondo Academy


A place where discipline, confidence, strength, and respect are built through the art of Taekwondo. Our academy is dedicated to training students of all ages with professional guidance, positive values, and a strong spirit of martial arts.

At Amdanga Taekwondo Academy, we believe Taekwondo is not only a sport but also a way of life. We focus on improving physical fitness, self-defense skills, mental strength, concentration, and self-confidence in every student. Our training helps students become disciplined, active, and responsible individuals both inside and outside the training ground.

Our academy encourages students to develop courage, respect, teamwork, and leadership qualities while representing the spirit of Taekwondo with pride. With passionate instructors and a supportive environment, Amdanga Taekwondo Academy continues to inspire young martial artists to achieve excellence and represent their academy with honor. Train with Discipline. Fight with Respect. Rise with Confidence.

TAEKWONDO PHILOSOPHY

The moral principles that guide every Taekwondo student.

Courtesy

Students learn to respect instructors, seniors, parents and fellow students. Courtesy creates humility, good behavior and a positive martial arts environment.

Integrity

Taekwondo teaches honesty and strong moral character. A true martial artist always chooses truth, fairness and responsibility in every situation.

Perseverance

Success requires patience and hard work. Students are trained to never give up during difficult situations, training or competitions.

Self Control

A martial artist must control emotions, anger and actions. Self-control helps students stay calm, disciplined and focused in life.

Indomitable Spirit

Taekwondo builds courage and confidence. Students learn to stand strong against fear, failure and challenges with determination.

Respect For Humanity

Martial arts should never be used for bullying or violence. Taekwondo promotes peace, kindness and helping others in society.

DISCIPLINE TRAINING

Discipline is the foundation of every successful martial artist.

Physical Discipline

Students follow regular warmups, stretching, stamina exercises and kicking drills to build strength, flexibility and endurance.

Mental Discipline

Taekwondo improves focus, patience and concentration. Students are trained to remain calm under pressure and think clearly.

Time Discipline

Punctuality is very important in martial arts. Students learn to attend classes regularly and maintain consistency in training.

Behavior Discipline

Respectful behavior inside and outside the dojang is mandatory. Students are expected to maintain good manners and positive attitudes.

Training Discipline

Students practice techniques repeatedly with dedication and patience to achieve perfection and improvement over time.

Life Discipline

The discipline learned in Taekwondo helps students succeed in school, career and personal life through confidence and responsibility.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Common questions students and parents ask before joining.

1. What is the minimum age to join?

Children from 5 years old can join beginner classes.

2. Can beginners join without experience?

Yes. Complete beginners are welcome in our academy.

3. Is Taekwondo safe for children?

Yes. Training is conducted under supervision with proper safety rules.

4. How many days per week should students train?

Students are encouraged to attend at least 3 classes per week for better progress.

5. Do girls and boys train together?

Yes. Both boys and girls train together with equal opportunities.

6. Is Taekwondo good for fitness?

Yes. It improves stamina, flexibility, balance and overall physical fitness.

7. How long does it take to earn a black belt?

It depends on regular practice and examination performance. Usually several years of consistent training are required.

8. Are competitions mandatory?

No. Competitions are optional, but students are encouraged to participate for experience and confidence.

9. What should students wear during training?

Students should wear a proper Taekwondo uniform (Dobok).

10. Does Taekwondo teach self-defense?

Yes. Students learn practical self-defense techniques and confidence building.

11. Are belt exams conducted regularly?

Yes. Belt examinations are organized according to student progress and performance.

12. Can parents watch the classes?

Yes. Parents may observe training sessions from designated areas.
